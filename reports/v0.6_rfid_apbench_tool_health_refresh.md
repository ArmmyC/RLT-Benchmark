## Historical Baseline Interpretation

The original baseline remains a valid historical record of generation and
extraction:

- generation completed: 15/15
- extraction passed and candidate available: 10/15
- empty responses: 4/15
- incomplete response rejected by extraction: 1/15

The original report recorded the other 10 rows as `compile_failure`. Bounded
failure analysis later established that the evaluator tools terminated before
candidate parsing because of a startup/runtime dependency problem. Under the
post-bugfix evaluator, that condition belongs to `tool_health_failed` or
`tool_startup_failure`, not confirmed candidate `compile_failure`.

Accordingly, the 10 old `compile_failure` labels in the historical Markdown,
CSV, and JSONL are superseded for interpretation by this report and
`reports/v0.6_rfid_apbench_3sample_failure_analysis.md`. They must not be used
as evidence that candidate RTL was syntactically rejected. No conclusion about
compile, simulation, synthesis, or score quality is available for those 10
candidates from the historical run.

## Refresh Decision

The reports were clarified, not regenerated. The current toolchain is
unhealthy, so regenerating downstream outcomes would only produce tool-health
blockers and could not establish candidate correctness or scoring.

The original baseline CSV and JSONL are intentionally unchanged. This
preserves the original v0.6 generated-output record, its sample identities, and
the labels emitted by the pre-bugfix evaluator. The prominent note in the
baseline Markdown and this companion report provide the required post-bugfix
interpretation.

## Next Operational Step

Before rerunning model scoring, restore the local runtime dependencies and
require all three tool health checks to pass, followed by successful public
reference validation and `reference_copy` candidate-fixture validation. Do not
rerun model scoring while `vvp` or Yosys remains `health_failed`.
