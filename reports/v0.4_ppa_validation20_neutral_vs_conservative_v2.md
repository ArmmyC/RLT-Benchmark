This report compares `conservative_area_v2` against the existing Stage L `neutral_baseline` artifact on the same deterministic 20-task public RTL-OPT slice.

- Benchmark: `rtlopt`
- Model: `qwen36-27b`
- Samples per task: 1
- Evaluator: `yosys_equiv`
- Scoring mode: `area_only`
- Area unit: `generic_cells`
- Toolchain ID: `yosys-generic-cell-count-proxy`
- Power: unavailable

The Yosys preflight passed before `conservative_area_v2` model calls:


`neutral_baseline` was not rerun; this comparison uses the existing Stage L neutral artifact.

No full RTL-OPT, `low_area_low_power`, `strict_rtl_only`, VerilogEval, RTLLM, ProtocolLLM, new-model, or private-task run was executed.

## Interpretation

`conservative_area_v2` substantially improves correctness-gated coverage versus both Stage L `conservative_area` and Stage L `neutral_baseline`: 17 valid records versus neutral's 14. It does not lose validity on any task where neutral is valid.

The tradeoff is area-proxy quality among valid records: v2's mean valid score is lower than neutral's, and one paired-valid task (`adder`) regresses. However, v2 also improves one paired-valid task (`comparator_16bit`) and has the higher all-sample mean because it keeps more tasks valid.

## Recommendation

Keep `conservative_area_v2` as the challenger for the next bounded validation, but do not promote it to full RTL-OPT or private SiliconCraft work yet. It is promising on validity, but the paired score regression on `adder` and lower mean valid score suggest one more bounded validation or prompt revision before scale-up.

## Caveats

`generic_cells` is a public-flow area proxy, not final silicon area.

Power metrics are unavailable. This report makes no measured power-improvement claim.

Timing is not being optimized or claimed.

This is still a small public validation, not a SiliconCraft private benchmark.
