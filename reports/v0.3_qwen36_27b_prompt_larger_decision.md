# v0.3 qwen36-27b Prompt Larger-Validation Decision

This report records the v0.3 prompt-profile decision after the larger validation slice for `qwen36-27b`.

No new benchmark results are introduced here. This decision uses the completed smoke summary, candidate decision report, and larger-validation summary.

## Recommendation

Keep `neutral_baseline` as the default prompt profile for now.

Keep `strict_rtl_only` as an optional challenger profile for future experiments.

Do not promote `low_area_low_power` or `no_explanation_code_only` from their held-back status.

The larger validation ended in a 31/40 vs 31/40 tie. `strict_rtl_only` was slightly better on VerilogEval, `neutral_baseline` was slightly better on RTL-OPT equivalence, and RTLLM was tied. Because there is no clear overall winner, the conservative choice is to keep the historical/control prompt as the default.

## Smoke Summary

The v0.3 smoke stage tested four prompt profiles across VerilogEval, RTLLM, and RTL-OPT with 3 samples per benchmark/profile.

| Prompt profile | VerilogEval | RTLLM | RTL-OPT | Smoke total | Smoke decision |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- |
| `neutral_baseline` | 3/3 | 2/3 | 2/3 | 7/9 | selected |
| `strict_rtl_only` | 3/3 | 2/3 | 2/3 | 7/9 | selected |
| `low_area_low_power` | 3/3 | 2/3 | 1/3 | 6/9 | held back |
| `no_explanation_code_only` | 3/3 | 1/3 | 1/3 | 5/9 | held back |

The candidate decision selected `neutral_baseline` and `strict_rtl_only` for larger validation because they tied for the best smoke total and had no compile failures. `low_area_low_power` was held back because it was weaker on RTL-OPT equivalence smoke. `no_explanation_code_only` was held back because it was weakest overall and had one RTLLM compile failure.

## Larger Validation Results

The larger validation compared only the selected candidates: `neutral_baseline` and `strict_rtl_only`.

| Prompt profile | VerilogEval passed | RTLLM passed | RTL-OPT passed | Total passed | Total samples | Recommendation |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- |
| `neutral_baseline` | 19 | 4 | 8 | 31 | 40 | keep default |
| `strict_rtl_only` | 20 | 4 | 7 | 31 | 40 | keep challenger |

Details:

- VerilogEval: `strict_rtl_only` passed 20/20, while `neutral_baseline` passed 19/20 with one compile failure.
- RTLLM: both profiles passed 4/10, with 5 compile failures and 1 simulation failure each.
- RTL-OPT equivalence: `neutral_baseline` passed 8/10, while `strict_rtl_only` passed 7/10.

## Tie Interpretation

The tied total means there is no strong evidence to switch the default prompt profile.

`strict_rtl_only` improved the VerilogEval larger slice by one task and removed the one VerilogEval compile failure seen under `neutral_baseline`. However, it did not improve RTLLM compile reliability, where both profiles had the same failure breakdown. It also lost one task on RTL-OPT equivalence.

`neutral_baseline` remains attractive as the default because it is the historical/control prompt, ties overall, and performs slightly better on RTL-OPT equivalence. RTL-OPT equivalence is especially important for optimization-oriented RTL work because syntax and synthesis alone are not enough to establish behavioral correctness.

## Held-Back Profiles

`low_area_low_power` remains held back. It is still a useful research hypothesis for future area/power-oriented studies, but the smoke evidence was weaker on RTL-OPT equivalence and this larger validation did not retest it.

`no_explanation_code_only` remains held back. It was weakest in smoke and had an RTLLM compile failure. This larger validation did not provide new evidence to promote it.

## Limitations

This is still a limited prompt experiment, not a final proof of prompt superiority. The larger slice is bigger than smoke but smaller than a full benchmark sweep.

The results do not prove final silicon PPA quality. Area, power, and timing claims require synthesis/PPA evidence and equivalence-preserving analysis.

The larger validation used matched conditions for the two selected prompt profiles, but stochastic generation can still vary across repeated runs. A future expansion can repeat or enlarge the comparison if a stronger decision is needed.

## Next Step

No immediate expansion is required. Use `neutral_baseline` as the default prompt profile for daily `qwen36-27b` RTL experiments, and keep `strict_rtl_only` available as a challenger when stricter output formatting is worth testing.

Further expansion is optional and should be done only if the team needs stronger evidence before changing the default.
